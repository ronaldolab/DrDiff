What is DrDiff

DrDiff is the name given to the Stochastic Drift-Diffusion Framework.

In the Protein-Folding context

Protein folding is a complex process that is usually described as diffusion over a one-dimensional projection of the high-dimensional energy landscape. The Stochastic Drift-Diffusion Framework (DrDiff) is an approach that characterizes the kinetic and thermodynamic properties of folding trajectories. DrDiff characterizes the thermodynamic free-energy profile (F(Q)) and the kinetic folding (τf) and transition path times (τTP) by determining the coordinate-dependent drift-velocity (v(Q)) and diffusion (D(Q)) coefficients from trajectory time traces (Q(t)).

With the rapid advance of single-molecule techniques, the DrDiff computational framework might be a useful ally for determining the free-energy, folding and transition path times by providing time observables of biological or condensed-phase systems.
